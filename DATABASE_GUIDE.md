# Yomu database guide

This document is the contract for humans and AI models extending Yomu's reading database.

## Files and targets

Data lives in `data/{course}-{level}.json`, where `course` is `hiragana` or `katakana` and `level` is `01`–`04`.

- Level 1 contains every supported single sign once. Its size is limited by the kana inventory.
- Levels 2–4 must contain at least 500 entries per course. Additions are welcome; never pad a file with duplicates or mechanically recombined templates.
- Hiragana and katakana are separate courses. Do not mechanically convert vocabulary from one script to the other.
- Level 4 katakana material may and should mix scripts naturally. Japanese grammar is normally written in hiragana.

Level 1 is generated by `python3 tools/generate_data.py`. Levels 2–4 are built from reviewed source exports by `python3 tools/build_large_corpus.py`. The latter expects JMdict, Tatoeba exports and Janome under `tmp/`; see the script's `require_files()` list. Commit generated JSON, but do not commit `tmp/`.

JMdict and Tatoeba source IDs form permanent card IDs. Never replace the content of an ID with an unrelated entry.

## JSON schema

```json
{
  "id": "hiragana02-001",
  "kana": "ねこ",
  "romaji": ["neko"],
  "kanji": "猫",
  "translation": "cat"
}
```

- `id`: required, permanent and unique across the whole database. Imported IDs derive from the source record, not selection order.
- `kana`: required. This is the question shown to the learner. Put normal ASCII spaces between words; the display setting may hide them.
- `romaji`: required non-empty array. The first value is the canonical answer displayed by Yomu. Further values are accepted alternatives.
- `kanji`: optional. Use the ordinary natural Japanese spelling, which may mix kanji and kana. Omit it when no useful kanji spelling exists.
- `translation`: required on levels 2–4 and written in clear natural English. Omit it on level 1.
- A future imported entry should also retain `source` and `sourceId` while it is being reviewed.

## Level rules

### Level 1 — single kana

Include basic kana, dakuten, handakuten and combinations such as `きゃ` / `キャ`. Do not include small `っ` / `ッ` or the long-vowel mark `ー` as standalone questions: neither has an independent reading.

### Level 2 — short real words

- Exactly 2–4 displayed kana characters after removing spaces.
- Must be a real, useful word with an English translation.
- Do not include small `っ` / `ッ` or `ー`.
- Prefer the script in which the word is naturally written: native vocabulary for hiragana and established loanwords/foreign names/technical vocabulary for katakana.
- Avoid archaic, offensive, highly specialized and ambiguous words.

### Level 3 — long words and short phrases

- A word of at least 5 kana, or a phrase of at most 4 words with at most 5 kana per word.
- Small `っ` / `ッ`, `ー`, contracted sounds and foreign katakana combinations are allowed.
- Phrases must be idiomatic, not arbitrary word combinations. Do not generate a Cartesian product of a short adjective and noun list.

### Level 4 — sentences

- A complete, natural Japanese sentence no longer than 30 characters including spaces and punctuation.
- Store a kana reading with spaces at useful word boundaries.
- `kanji` must contain the normal Japanese orthography, not an all-kanji transformation.
- Katakana-course sentences should contain meaningful katakana reading practice, while particles and inflections stay naturally in hiragana.
- Translation must match the Japanese sentence without adding context that is not present.

## Romaji policy

The displayed canonical form follows modified Hepburn (`shi`, `chi`, `tsu`, `fu`, `ji`). Yomu ignores case, spaces, hyphens, apostrophes and common punctuation when checking.

- Accept macrons and plain keyboard spellings for long vowels where applicable: for example `tōkyō` and `toukyou`.
- The canonical answer should be typeable on an ordinary Latin keyboard.
- Particles are read by pronunciation: `は` → `wa`, `へ` → `e`, `を` → `o`.
- At the end of an answer, Yomu accepts both the written and commonly devoiced forms of `desu` and `masu`: `desu` / `des`, `masu` / `mas`.
- Do not add an alternative unless it is a legitimate romanization or a conventional keyboard representation. Never accept a genuinely different pronunciation merely to be lenient.

## Review checklist

Before adding or importing an entry, verify all of the following:

1. A native dictionary or trustworthy corpus supports the Japanese form and meaning.
2. The script is natural for the selected course.
3. Kana, kanji, romaji and translation describe exactly the same reading and sense.
4. The entry obeys its level's length and character rules.
5. Spaces occur only at useful word boundaries.
6. The canonical romaji is correct, including particles, gemination and long vowels.
7. There is no duplicate question or duplicate ID in the target file.
8. A sentence is self-contained, grammatical and natural without hidden context.
9. Run `python3 tools/generate_data.py` and `python3 tools/validate_data.py`.

For levels 2–4, run `python3 tools/build_large_corpus.py` before the validator. The builder cross-checks Tatoeba's reviewed kana transcription against Janome's reading, limits near-duplicate character shingles, and caps repeated content lemmas.

## Using JMdict and Tatoeba

JMdict and Tatoeba are sources of candidates, not automatic publishers.

- For JMdict, retain common contemporary senses; reject names, archaisms, rare spellings and entries whose intended sense cannot be selected confidently.
- For Tatoeba, prefer directly linked English translations, approved Japanese sentences owned by native speakers, and non-orphan entries. Record the source sentence IDs.
- Every imported candidate still needs the checklist above. Automated filters cannot establish pedagogical level or naturalness on their own.
- Respect source licenses and preserve attribution metadata in the import pipeline.
